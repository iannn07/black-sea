# Lane: Competitor & Market Intelligence

For "competitor analysis," "market/positioning," "threat to us," "moat." Pair with `collection.md`
and `analytic-tradecraft.md`. This is decision-support: the reader wants to *act* (price, enter,
defend, invest), so tie findings to implications.

## A. Frameworks (use as lenses, not checklists)

- **Five Forces** — rivalry, new entrants, substitutes, supplier power, buyer power. Rate each and
  say where the profit pool actually sits.
- **Value chain / cost structure** — where the competitor makes and spends money; where they're
  vulnerable.
- **Positioning map** — plot players on the two axes that matter to *this* market (price/quality,
  breadth/depth, etc.), not generic ones.
- **SWOT — earn it.** Only include items that are sourced and decision-relevant; a SWOT of
  guesses is noise.

## B. Competitor deep-dive (per target)

- **Corporate:** ownership, funding, financial health (→ org-financial lane if numbers are available).
- **Product/pricing:** line-up, pricing model and recent moves, roadmap signals (job posts, patents,
  conference talks, changelogs).
- **Go-to-market:** channels, key accounts, partnerships, marketing posture.
- **Capabilities:** hiring patterns (what roles = what bets), tech stack, capacity, supply chain.
- **Leadership & strategy signals:** exec background, public statements, M&A history.

## C. Market analysis

- **Sizing** — TAM/SAM/SOM. Show the method (top-down vs. bottom-up) and inputs; give a **range**
  with assumptions, never a fake-precise single number.
- **Segmentation & demand drivers** — who buys, why, what moves the curve.
- **Trends & disruption** — regulatory, technological, substitution; who benefits.
- **Structure** — concentration, share estimates (with confidence), switching costs, network effects.

## D. Moat & threat assessment

Classify durability of advantage: intangibles/brand, switching costs, network effects, cost
advantage, efficient scale. State which are real and defensible vs. asserted. Then the punchline the
reader needs: **how threatening is this competitor to the user's position, and on what timeline** —
with indicators to watch (an "indicators & warning" list: pricing moves, hires, filings that would
signal the threat materializing).

## E. Ethical CI boundary

Competitive intelligence is open-source and honest. **No misrepresentation** to elicit information
(posing as a customer/journalist/job-seeker to extract secrets), no soliciting anyone to break an
NDA, no trade-secret theft. All findings come from public/observable sources. This isn't just ethics
— fruit from those methods is legally toxic and destroys the product's usability. Keep it clean.

## F. Deliverable block ("Competitor Posture" / "Market")

Findings tied to **implications and recommended watch-indicators**, each confidence-graded and
sourced. Separate what's observed from what's inferred about intent (intent is an assessment — grade
it). Put your market-size method and assumptions in the open so the reader can pressure-test them.

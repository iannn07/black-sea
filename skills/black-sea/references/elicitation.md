# Source Elicitation (PARLEY)

The lawful art of getting information from **people who have it** — through planned, honest
conversation and structured interviewing, not deception. Pair with `analytic-tradecraft.md` (grade
what you're told) and `collection.md`.

**Baseline.** This lane is modelled on the documented, *legal* competitive-intelligence elicitation
tradition — principally **John Nolan** (founder of the Phoenix Consulting Group; his *Confidential*,
HarperCollins, 1999, is the standard operational text), whose core teaching is that a **well-planned
conversation is the most valuable collection tool**, and that the skill is knowing *who* is likely to
know and *how* to have that conversation legitimately. Where a specific conversational lever below is
general public elicitation-awareness canon rather than a claim traceable to a named source, treat it
as technique, not gospel — and never let technique become misrepresentation.

## 1. Plan the conversation (most of the work is here)

- **Fix the gap.** What exactly do you need to learn? An unplanned chat leaks more than it gathers.
- **Find who knows.** Rarely the obvious insider — often the periphery: former employees, suppliers,
  distributors, conference speakers, industry analysts, standards-body participants, regulators.
- **Pick the venue.** Trade shows and conferences are, in Nolan's account, unparalleled: people are
  there to talk about their work, openly, to strangers.
- **Prepare honestly.** Know enough to ask good questions and recognise a real answer from a brush-off.

## 2. Conduct it (legitimate technique)

- **Rapport and genuine interest** — people talk to those who listen well.
- **Open questions, then silence** — ask, then stop; people fill silence.
- **Reciprocity** — offering your own *non-sensitive*, shareable perspective invites the same back.
- **Documented human tendencies** — people tend to correct an error, to confirm or complete a partial
  picture, and to want to appear knowledgeable and helpful. These are levers of *ordinary
  conversation*; using them is not deception. Misstating *who you are* to trigger them is.
- **Active listening and follow-through** — the second and third question, built on the answer, get
  more than the first.

## 3. Structured & reference interviewing

For named, willing sources — reference checks, expert/SME calls, subject interviews — use a structured
approach: neutral open prompts, chronological walk-throughs (the cognitive-interview pattern), no
leading questions, and separate what the source *knows* from what they *believe* or *heard*.

## 4. Grade what you hear, read for evasion

A source is a source — grade it (Admiralty). Note motive to deceive or impress; corroborate with an
independent origin before a spoken claim becomes anything above **cold**. Watch for evasion, hedging,
and answers that are too clean.

## The hard legal line (this is what makes it PARLEY, not pretexting)

Legitimate elicitation and social-engineering fraud use overlapping conversational skill; the
difference is **honesty about who you are and what you may take**. PARLEY may hold an honest
conversation and need not always volunteer its full commercial purpose — but it must **never**:

- Misrepresent identity or affiliation, impersonate anyone, or invent a false pretext (posing as a
  customer, journalist, job-seeker, student, or regulator to extract information).
- Induce anyone to breach an **NDA**, employment duty, or the law.
- Seek privileged, classified, insider-trading, or otherwise protected information.
- Record or intercept where doing so is unlawful without consent.

If a fact requires any of the above to obtain, it is a **gap**, not a task — write it down and stop.
Person-directed work still passes HARBORMASTER's legitimate-purpose gate.

## Deliverable (PARLEY findings packet)

Return per the operative contract: graded findings from each conversation (source, Admiralty grade,
what it supports, motive-to-deceive note), entities discovered, and named **gaps** — including facts
that were out of reach *because* the lawful line held. Note that spoken, uncorroborated claims stay
**cold** until an independent origin confirms them.

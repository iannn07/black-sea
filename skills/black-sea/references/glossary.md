# Glossary — BLACK SEA leading words

Compact terms used exactly throughout the skill. Consistent language = predictable runs. Use these
words rather than restating their meaning; that's what makes them earn their place.

- **Prime Directive** — never fabricate. Every finding traces to a real source or is labeled
  assessment/assumption. The pull to "fill in" a plausible detail is the signal to write a *gap*.
- **BLUF** — Bottom Line Up Front. The 3–5 sentence answer to the tasking question + overall
  confidence, before any detail.
- **gap** — a fact you could not lawfully obtain this session. Named, with a next-collection step.
  A named gap is a professional result; a filled-in guess is fabrication.
- **cold** — a finding with no independent corroboration. Stays cold (low confidence) until a second,
  separate-origin source confirms it. Opposite: **confirmed**.
- **clean** — collection obtained without breaking a law or a terms-of-service. Anything that would
  require pretexting, unauthorized access, or stolen data is not clean — it's a *gap*, not a task.
- **the seam** — where anomalies live: shared officers/addresses/phones, impossible timelines,
  round-number financials, metadata that contradicts a claim. Look here first.
- **FLASH / FULL** — the two output tiers. FLASH = compressed single-screen read. FULL = complete
  standardized dossier. FLASH is *shorter*, never *sloppier*.
- **FACT / ASSESSMENT / ASSUMPTION** — the three labels every claim carries. Sourced / reasoned from
  facts / unverified premise. Never blurred.
- **Admiralty grade** — the two-axis source rating (reliability A–F, credibility 1–6) on every entry
  in the Evidence Register. See `analytic-tradecraft.md`.
- **ACH** — Analysis of Competing Hypotheses. Test evidence against *all* plausible explanations,
  weighting disconfirmation hardest. The innocent explanation is always a required hypothesis.
- **codename** — the all-caps case designation (e.g. `OP. MERIDIAN`). All entities referred to by
  codename in the vault, per NIGHTSTALKER.
- **the register** — the Evidence Register (Annex A): source-by-source provenance and grade. Every
  FACT points into it.
- **the unit / orchestrator** — BLACK SEA itself: fires on investigation intent, runs WAYPOINT, then
  dispatches to operatives and assembles + delivers the dossier.
- **operative** — a specialist the orchestrator dispatches to: **DRY DOCK** (ownership/shell),
  **PLIMSOLL** (statements/back-test), **HARBORMASTER** (person), **HORIZON** (competitor/market),
  **GRASSHOPPER** (link/network), **PARLEY** (source elicitation). The three **lanes** are graduating
  into operatives; until each has its own skill, a lane is reached by a context pointer to its
  reference file.
- **WAYPOINT** — the front-door operative: interviews the operator, converts the tasking into an
  intelligence question, decomposes it into EEI, and emits the Collection Plan. Sets the heading; does
  not collect.
- **EEI** (Essential Elements of Information) — the tiered information requirements a tasking
  decomposes into: **specified** (literally asked), **implied** (required by the objective though
  unstated), **adjacent** (analyst-improvised, each justified). Adjacent EEI are questions to pursue,
  never answers asserted.
- **Collection Plan** — WAYPOINT's operator-approved output: codename, tier, intelligence question,
  scope, the EEI table, and anticipated gaps. No collection begins until it is approved.
- **findings packet** — the standard output every operative returns: graded findings, an
  Evidence-Register fragment, discovered entities, named gaps, and an optional lane block. See
  `operative-contract.md`.
- **doctrine floor** — the three invariants every operative carries even when invoked alone: label
  FACT/ASSESSMENT/ASSUMPTION; grade every source (Admiralty); name every gap.

# Lane: Person Profiling

For due diligence on an individual: a company principal, a counterparty, an executive, a public
figure, or a named subject inside a broader case. Pair with `collection.md` and
`analytic-tradecraft.md`.

This lane carries the heaviest guardrails in BLACK SEA, because the same techniques that make
legitimate due diligence also make surveillance. The gate below is not optional.

## GATE 0 — Legitimate purpose & lawful source (run before any person collection)

Proceed only if **both** hold. If either fails, stop and say why.

1. **Legitimate purpose.** There is a defensible reason a reasonable professional would run this:
   due diligence on a business counterparty/principal/hire, vetting a public figure, fraud/AML
   investigation, journalism, litigation support. Purposes that fail: tracking, stalking,
   harassment, intimidation, settling a personal score, or building leverage over a private person.
2. **Public figure or a person genuinely in a case.** Profiling of a **private individual** with no
   legitimate case nexus is out of scope — decline it. If the subject is a **minor**, stop: no
   profiling of children.

If the request smells like locating, monitoring, or exposing a private person, treat that as the
answer — decline, don't reframe it into something that sounds acceptable.

**Lawful-source discipline.** Public records and lawfully public information only. Be mindful that
regulated uses (employment, credit, tenant, insurance screening) fall under regimes like the FCRA and
similar laws worldwide — this skill produces investigative intelligence, **not** a regulated consumer
report, and you should say so. No consumer-database misuse, no pretexting, no accessing non-public
records, no leaked/breached PII (exposure-flagging only, per collection.md).

## Profile structure (public/lawful sources only)

- **Identity & resolution** — establish you have the *right* person; disambiguate common names using
  corroborating identifiers, not guesses. Misidentification is the cardinal error here.
- **Professional footprint** — roles, companies, directorships/officerships (→ registries), licenses,
  credentials, publications, patents.
- **Corporate & financial nexus** — entities they control or are officers of; sanctions/PEP/debarment
  hits; public litigation involving them in a business capacity.
- **Public statements & media** — adverse-media screening (litigation, enforcement, credible
  reporting), interviews, conference talks. Grade tabloid/anonymous sources low.
- **Public digital footprint** — professional profiles and clearly public posts, read at altitude for
  reputation/consistency signals. Note metadata/exposure per collection.md. Do not assemble a
  minute-by-minute pattern-of-life; that's surveillance, not diligence.

## Cardinal rules

- **Identity confidence is a finding.** State how sure you are it's the same person, and never merge
  two people's records without corroboration.
- **Public capacity, not private life.** Report what bears on the legitimate question (their business
  conduct, integrity, competence, conflicts) — not their private movements, relationships, health, or
  home, which are neither in scope nor lawful to compile.
- **Grade hard, corroborate harder.** Reputational claims about a person demand A/B-grade, independent
  corroboration before they go in at any real confidence. One hostile anonymous source is E5, not a finding.

## Deliverable block ("Subject Profile")

BLUF the answer to the diligence question (integrity/competence/conflict/risk). Findings
confidence-graded and sourced, with an explicit **identity-confidence** line. Adverse findings held to
the strictest sourcing bar. Collection Gaps stated. Add the caveat that this is investigative
intelligence from public sources, not a regulated background/consumer report, and not a basis for any
FCRA-regulated decision on its own.
